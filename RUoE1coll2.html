The Corporate Whistleblower


When Sarah discovered the financial irregularities, she was disappointed by her company's behaviour. The decision to report the fraud would inevitably an impact on her career, but she felt morally obligated to act. Her supervisor tried to convince her that exposing the scandal was of her business and that she should focus on her own responsibilities.

Despite the pressure, Sarah decided to research into the company's financial practices. She spent weeks collecting evidence, often working late into the night. The investigation was putting on her personal life, but she was determined to see it through. Eventually, she contacted a journalist who agreed to her identity a secret while investigating the story.

The article was published several months later and an example to other potential whistleblowers about the importance of speaking out. The investigation and subsequent reforms place over several years - the company was investigated, several executives were prosecuted, and new regulations were implemented to prevent similar incidents in the future.

Answer Key & Explanations

1. C) bitterly (bitterly disappointed)
2. A) have (have an impact)
3. D) none (none of her business)
4. A) do (do research)
5. A) pressure (put pressure on)
6. A) keep (keep her identity a secret)
7. A) set (set an example)
8. B) took (took place)
