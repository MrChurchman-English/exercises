Grammar Practice: Advanced Future Tenses

Instructions: Complete the sentences using the correct future form of the verb in brackets. Use either the Future Continuous, Future Perfect Simple, or Future Perfect Continuous. Use contractions where appropriate (e.g., won't), though full forms are also accepted.
1. Don't phone me between 8:00 and 9:00 PM tonight because I (watch) the championship match.
2. By the time the professor finishes his lecture, the students (listen) to him for over three hours.
3. I've already bought the materials, so by this weekend, I (build) the new garden shed.
4. This time next week, we (sunbathe) on a beautiful beach in Bali!
5. Call me at 6:00 PM. I (arrive) home from work by then.
6. By December, Sarah (work) at this company for exactly ten years.
7. Please don't come over at midnight. I (sleep) soundly by that time.
8. If Mark doesn't speed up, the movie (start) before he even reaches the cinema.
9. Next month, my parents (be married) for thirty years.
10. At 3:00 PM tomorrow, the engineers (test) the new software system.
11. By midnight, the novelists (write) for twelve consecutive hours to meet their deadline.
12. Do you think you (finish) writing the annual report by Friday morning?
13. Don't worry about finding us; we (wear) bright red jackets so you can spot us easily.
14. When the clock strikes noon, the chefs (cook) the banquet dinner for five hours.
15. Hopefully, scientists (find) a definitive cure for the disease by the end of the decade.
